**Short answer:** A complete Halifax SEO checklist runs in four phases — technical foundations, Google Business Profile, on-page optimisation, and local link building — worked through in that order. Fix your site's speed and crawlability first, optimise your profile and reviews second, build out keyword-targeted pages third, and earn local links last. Below is the full 25-step sequence you can follow start to finish.

This is the practical companion to everything else in our [Halifax SEO](/) library — a single ordered list so you always know your next move. Work top to bottom; each phase builds on the one before.

## Phase 1: Technical Foundations

Google won't rank a broken site, so start here.

1. **Run a speed test** and fix anything dragging your load time above 2.5 seconds.
2. **Confirm mobile-friendliness** — most Halifax searches are on phones.
3. **Install HTTPS** if you haven't; HTTP sites get penalised.
4. **Submit an XML sitemap** in Google Search Console.
5. **Check your robots.txt** isn't blocking important pages.
6. **Fix broken links and 404s** across the site.
7. **Add LocalBusiness schema markup** with your Halifax address and services.

For the reasoning behind each of these, see our complete guide on [how to rank on Google in Halifax](/blog/how-to-rank-on-google-in-halifax/).

## Phase 2: Google Business Profile

Your fastest source of local leads.

8. **Claim and verify** your Google Business Profile.
9. **Complete every field** — hours, phone, website, service area, attributes.
10. **Choose the most specific primary category** that matches your business.
11. **Add 20+ photos** and commit to new ones monthly.
12. **Set up a review system** with a one-tap review link.
13. **Respond to every review**, old and new, within a day or two.
14. **Post updates** twice a month to keep the profile active.

The full walkthrough is in our [Halifax Google Business Profile guide](/blog/halifax-google-business-profile-guide/).

## Phase 3: On-Page Optimisation

Now make each page earn its rankings.

15. **Do keyword research** to find local, commercial-intent terms — our guide to [SEO keywords for Halifax businesses](/blog/seo-keywords-for-halifax-businesses/) shows how.
16. **Optimise your homepage** for your main "[service] Halifax" keyword.
17. **Build one page per core service**, not a single catch-all page.
18. **Create location pages** for the HRM communities you serve, each with unique content.
19. **Write keyword-driven titles and H1s** that include your service and location.
20. **Add internal links** between related pages so authority flows through your site.
21. **Start a blog** targeting long-tail Halifax questions your customers ask.

## Phase 4: Local Link Building

The signal that pushes you past competitors.

22. **Build consistent citations** — identical Name, Address, Phone everywhere online.
23. **Get listed in Halifax directories** like the Chamber of Commerce and Discover Halifax.
24. **Earn local media and partner links** through coverage, sponsorships, and relationships.
25. **Create linkable assets** — local guides or data that other Halifax sites want to reference.

## How to Use This Checklist

Don't try to do all 25 at once. Work through them in order, because each phase strengthens the next: technical fixes make your content rank better, an optimised profile compounds with every review, and links amplify pages that are already well-built.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even businesses that work the checklist trip on a few predictable things. Watch for these:

- **Building location pages with duplicated content.** Google spots a template with the city name swapped out. Each Dartmouth, Bedford, or Sackville page needs genuinely unique, locally relevant copy.
- **Targeting broad keywords you can't win.** "Best plumber" competes with the world; "emergency plumber Halifax" competes with a dozen local firms. Stay local and specific.
- **Treating SEO as a one-time project.** Rankings decay as competitors keep publishing and earning links. The checklist is a cycle you revisit, not a task you finish once.
- **Neglecting reviews after the initial push.** Review velocity matters — a steady trickle of recent reviews outranks a one-time burst that's now two years old.

## Track Your Progress

As you work the list, watch three things in Google Search Console and your Google Business Profile dashboard: impressions (are you appearing for more searches?), average position (are you climbing?), and actions (calls, clicks, direction requests). Movement in these confirms the checklist is working — usually within 30 to 90 days.

Set a recurring monthly reminder to review these numbers and revisit the checklist from the top. SEO rewards consistency more than intensity: a business that completes two checklist items every month for a year will comfortably outrank one that did a frantic week of work and then went quiet.
